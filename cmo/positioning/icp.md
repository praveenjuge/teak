# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Who is Teak for? Who should we prioritize?**

---

## Primary Persona: The Visual Creative

### Profile Overview

**Name:** The Design-Driven Creative

**Mindset:** "I consume inspiration constantly. I need to capture it without breaking my flow, and find it when I need it."

---

### Demographics

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Age** | 25-40 |
| **Role** | Designer, Developer, Creative Director, Marketer, Founder |
| **Industry** | Tech, Design, Creative Agencies, Startups |
| **Tech Comfort** | Highâ€”power user, comfortable with new tools |
| **Income** | $50k-$150k+ (willing to pay for tools that save time) |

---

### Behaviors

**Digital Habits:**
- Heavy consumer of online content (Dribbble, Twitter/X, blogs, YouTube)
- Saves things constantly with intent to use "later"
- Has tried multiple productivity/bookmarking tools
- Values aesthetics and user experience
- Mobile-first lifestyle

**Work Patterns:**
- Works on multiple projects simultaneously
- Needs to reference past inspiration for new projects
- Collaborates with teams but has personal workflow
- Often works across multiple devices

**Pain Behaviors:**
- Takes screenshots because bookmarks aren't visual enough
- Pastes links into Slack/DMs to self to "save" them
- Has bookmark folders like "To Read" that are ignored
- Googles things they know they've saved before
- Feels guilty about not using saved content

### Jobs to Be Done

**Functional Jobs:**
1. Save inspiration quickly without context-switching
2. Find saved content later with minimal effort
3. Organize without manual maintenance
4. Access saved content from any device

**Emotional Jobs:**
1. Feel confident that nothing good will be lost
2. Feel professional and organized
3. Reduce anxiety about "forgetting" good ideas

**Social Jobs:**
1. Share inspiration easily with teams
2. Look competent to clients/colleagues

---

### Goals

**Primary Goals:**
- Build a personal library of inspiration that grows in value
- Reduce time spent searching for things they know they have
- Capture more without more effort
- Maintain creative momentum

**Secondary Goals:**
- Share inspiration with teams
- Establish a personal knowledge system
- Support continuous learning and growth

---

### Success Metrics

**They'll know Teak is working when:**
- They actually find things they saved weeks/months ago
- They save more because it's frictionless
- They feel relieved, not stressed, about their saved content
- They recommend it to colleagues organically

**Mindset:** "I need to capture ideas, research competitors, and save referencesâ€”and I want to own my data."

| **Industry** | SaaS, Consumer Apps, Developer Tools |

### Why They Love Teak

**Technical Appeal:**
- Open source (can self-host, verify privacy)
- Modern stack (Next.js, Convex, React Native)
- Privacy-first (not another VC-owned data harvest)
- Extensible platform potential

**Practical Appeal:**
- Capture competitor research
- Save feature ideas from across the web
- Build visual mood boards for projects
- Reference past decisions and inspiration

## Firmographics (if B2B)

### Target Company Types

**Sweet Spot:**
- Design agencies (5-50 employees)
- Product teams at tech companies
- Creative teams at startups
- Marketing teams at SaaS companies

**Why Teams, Not Just Individuals?**
- Shared inspiration library
- Onboarding new team members
- Maintaining brand consistency
- Competitive intelligence

**Team Buying Signals:**
- Multiple users from same domain signing up
- Requests for collaboration features
- Questions about team pricing
- Enterprise/data privacy questions
