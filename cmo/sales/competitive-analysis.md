## Deep Dive: Key Competitors

### 1. Notion

**What it is:** All-in-one workspace for notes, docs, projects

**Why people choose it:**
- Familiar, document-based
- Great for collaboration
- Extensible with databases

**Why they leave it:**
- Too slow for quick capture
- Requires manual organization
- Visual content looks bad
- Search is overwhelming
- "I never go back to what I save"

**Teak wins when:**
- You need to capture quickly without friction
- You want visual-first browsing
- You don't want to manually organize everything

**Migration path:** Import Notion bookmarks, use Teak for inspiration, keep Notion for docs

---

### 2. Obsidian

**What it is:** Local-first, markdown-based knowledge base with graph view

**Why people choose it:**
- Local storage (privacy)
- Extensible with plugins
- Graph view is cool
- Markdown is future-proof

**Why they leave it:**
- Text-first, no native media support
- Plugin management is a chore
- No mobile-native experience
- Requires constant maintenance
- Steep learning curve

**Teak wins when:**
- You save images, videos, not just text
- You want AI to do the organizing
- You want native mobile apps
- You don't want to tinker with plugins

**Complementary:** Use Obsidian for deep knowledge work, Teak for visual inspiration

---

### 3. Pocket

**What it is:** "Read later" app with articles and videos

**Why people choose it:**
- Simple, focused on reading
- Good mobile apps
- Clean reading experience

**Why they leave it:**
- Only for articles, not images/designs
- Terrible at rediscovery
- No organization beyond tags
- Feels like a graveyard of unread content

**Teak wins when:**
- You save more than just articles
- You want visual browsing
- You need powerful search
- You save images/designs more than text

---
